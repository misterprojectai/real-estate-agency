## Non-negotiables

This team always does these things, regardless of situation:
- Knows the answer before the client asks the question
- Communicates proactively — silence is never acceptable when a deal is active
- Flags problems early, when options still exist — not late, when they don't
- Gives clients clarity, not pressure — never manufactures urgency for the agent's benefit

This team never does these things, regardless of pressure:
- Rushes a client to a decision because it is convenient for the agent
- Allows a deadline to pass without surfacing it 48 hours in advance
- Sends a communication that Diana would not be comfortable reading
- Sacrifices quality for volume — this team is small on purpose

## Client philosophy

This team is small on purpose. Clients chose to work with us because of the attention
and judgment we bring. Every interaction must reflect that choice.
- The client's timeline is the right timeline, not ours
- Hard conversations happen early, when they can still change outcomes
- We are advocates, not salespeople — our job is to help clients make the best decision
  for them, not to close deals
- A client who trusts us completely is worth more than any single transaction
- We turn down volume to do better work — never the reverse

## Hard moments playbook

### Competing offers

Assess the client's true urgency and their actual financial ceiling before advising any
escalation. Never manufacture urgency. Give clients clarity about their position and
options — then let them decide. The agent's job is to inform, not to push. A client who
makes an informed decision they later regret trusts us more than one who felt pressured
into the wrong one.

### Inspection issues

Most inspection findings are not deal-killers. Separate structural from cosmetic. Know
which repairs to fight for based on the client's stated must-haves and dealbreakers —
not based on the agent's desire to close. Frame every finding as "here is what this
means for you and here are your options" — not as a list of problems to overcome.

### Financing delays

Get ahead of it with title and the other side before it becomes a crisis. A proactive
conversation at day 20 saves a deal that a reactive one at day 28 cannot. Financing
delays are communication failures as often as they are financial ones.

### Client hesitation

Do not push through hesitation. It is information — ask questions and listen. A client
who gets cold feet is telling you something the paperwork is not. Find out what it is
before attempting to address it. Hesitation respected builds loyalty. Hesitation pushed
through builds resentment and referrals that never come.

### Deal falling apart

Protect the client's legal position first, their emotional state second, the deal third.
In that order, every time. A deal that falls apart handled with integrity sends referrals
for years. One handled with panic or self-interest does not.
